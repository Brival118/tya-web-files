TYA Music Project

A unique opportunity for young people to work with highly experienced musicians and write their own song which is then recorded. The course takes place over 5 days in school holidays. Dealing with issues that directly affect young people, the participants develop their own lyrics and together with songwriters from All over the world , their ideas become a reality and a new song is created.

TYA Information TechnologyProject

Working with our partners. This 3 day course enables students to learn about computer infrastructure, digital media and programming from the experts in their field. The course is conducted in a safe, friendly environment, enabling young people to gain a real understanding of IT and future careers while achieving accredited awards.

TY AEmployability program

In collaboration with local companies from a variety of different industries, the 5-week TYA Employability Programme is designed to prepare young people for the workplace. The course aims to help a group of students discover their next steps after school leading them to a promising and rewarding career. We will work with a select number of school students( High school - university)to:

Develop their key skills such as communication and research skills.

Increase thier self esteem and confidence.

Improve their general well being.

explore different career paths and industries.

equipe them with the resources they need to find and apply for positions.

Introduce them to ksy personnel from local organisations, creaating valuable future contacts.

Experience different working environments.

Student will complete careers and experiencing work awards while taking part in the program.

TYA Sports and Fitness program

A 4-day programme dedicated to improving sports and fitness skills while ensuring that young people stay safe. Working with our partners International Sports Athletes and other local providers students will develop their skills in tennis, football and athletics. The course includes competitions and an introduction to clubs in schools so that students are able to continue their involvement at the completion of the project. Students will receive accredited awards.
